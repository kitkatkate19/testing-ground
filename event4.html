ETO Hong Kong 2025
Finals Day

The culminating day of KBI HK's second Education Technology Olympiad — 18 finalist teams, three competition tracks, and a packed auditorium at HKUST. Here's what happened.

21 June 2025
HKUST, Clear Water Bay
18 finalist teams · 300+ attendees
Event Recap

What Happened

ETO Hong Kong 2025 Finals Day brought together the top 18 teams from across the competition's three tracks — IBCOL (blockchain), IDSOL (data science), and IQCOL (quantum computing) — for a full-day showcase at HKUST's Shaw Auditorium. From 9 AM to 6 PM, teams presented final projects to a panel of 12 judges drawn from academia, financial services, and technology.

The atmosphere was distinctly different from qualifying rounds: more polished, more competitive, and considerably more public. Teams from 23 secondary schools and 8 universities competed alongside each other in a format that deliberately mixed ISCED levels — exposing school-age participants to the rigour of university-standard presentations, and challenging university teams to communicate with clarity to a broader audience.

Finals Day was also the largest KBI HK event to date by attendance, with over 300 students, educators, industry professionals, and alumni filling the auditorium across the day.

Competition Results

2025 Winners

🥇
Team Meridian — IBCOL Track
HKUST · Students Stream
Winner for their project "Transparent Supply Chain Provenance via Layer-2 Attestation" — a working prototype demonstrating real-time product authenticity verification for the pharmaceutical sector using zero-knowledge proofs on Ethereum L2.
🥇
Team Apex — IDSOL Track
CUHK · Students Stream
Winner for "Predictive Urban Heat Island Modelling Using Multi-Source Sensor Fusion" — a data pipeline combining satellite imagery, IoT sensor networks, and gradient-boosted models to forecast urban temperature anomalies for city planning use.
🥇
Team Quanta — IQCOL Track
HKU · Students Stream
Winner for "Variational Quantum Eigensolver for Molecular Ground State Estimation" — a research paper and Qiskit implementation demonstrating VQE on IBM Quantum hardware to estimate ground state energies of small molecules relevant to drug discovery.
🏅
Best Schoolers Project — IBCOL Track
St. Paul's College · Schoolers Stream
"Blockchain for School Records: A Privacy-First Approach" — awarded Best Schoolers Project for exceptional clarity of problem definition, originality of solution, and quality of presentation from a team of three Form 5 students.
Keynote & Judges

Featured Speakers

Dr. Michelle Lam
Keynote Speaker · Research Fellow in Distributed Systems, HKUST
Delivered the opening keynote: "The Next Decade of Distributed Systems — What Students Need to Know." Dr. Lam challenged participants to think beyond current blockchain architectures and consider the convergence of zero-knowledge proofs with post-quantum cryptography.
Arjun Sharma
Lead Judge, IBCOL Track · Blockchain Engineer
Led the IBCOL judging panel and delivered post-award feedback to all competing teams. Arjun praised the overall standard as markedly higher than ETO 2024's inaugural edition, noting that "the gap between schoolers and students teams has narrowed significantly."
Looking Ahead

ETO Hong Kong 2026 is now open for registration. Building on the success of the 2025 edition, KBI HK has expanded the competition to include additional mentorship sessions, a new collaborative project category, and enhanced support for Schoolers Stream teams.
