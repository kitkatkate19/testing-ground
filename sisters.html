Sibling Organisations

KBI Hong Kong is part of a broader ecosystem. These are the organisations we collaborate with, share values with, and consider part of our extended community.

About This Page

We Don't Work Alone

Building a technology education ecosystem in Hong Kong requires collaboration — not just within KBI HK, but with the wider organisations that share our commitment to rigour, inclusion, and student development. This page introduces the organisations we consider sibling groups: those who are affiliated with the KBI network, aligned with our values, or actively partnering with us on specific programmes.

If your organisation believes you should be listed here, or if you would like to explore a formal affiliation with KBI HK, please get in touch.

Featured Sibling Organisation

Ladies of Lovelace

Empowering women and girls in emerging technology — education, mentorship, and community

KBI-Affiliated Organisation
Hong Kong
Active — Events & Mentorship

Ladies of Lovelace is a Hong Kong-based organisation dedicated to increasing the participation, visibility, and advancement of women and girls in emerging technology fields. Named after Ada Lovelace — widely recognised as the world's first computer programmer — the organisation builds community, mentorship structures, and educational programming for women across secondary schools, universities, and the professional technology sector.

KBI HK and Ladies of Lovelace share a commitment to rigorous, non-commercial technology education and to ensuring that Hong Kong's emerging-technology ecosystem is genuinely diverse and inclusive. We collaborate on joint events, support each other's programme participants, and share relevant opportunities with our respective communities.

Ladies of Lovelace runs its own event series, mentorship matching programme, and community gatherings — separate from KBI HK's competition and certification programmes, but complementary to them. We encourage KBI HK members to explore Ladies of Lovelace's programming, and vice versa.

Cross-Organisation Collaboration

How We Work with
Sibling Organisations

Our relationships with sibling organisations are based on shared values, not formal agreements alone. We collaborate in ways that make sense for participants — not just for institutional optics. Typical forms of collaboration include:

Joint events — co-organised workshops, panels, or networking sessions that serve both communities

Cross-promotion — sharing each other's relevant opportunities with our respective member communities

Mentorship pathways — connecting participants across organisations to broaden mentorship access

Resource sharing — jointly developing educational materials, speaker networks, and programme resources

Propose a Partnership

Is your organisation working in the technology education space in Hong Kong or globally? If you share our values of rigour, inclusion, and student development, we would like to hear from you. We approach each potential partnership on its merits — the alignment of values matters more than the size or resources of the organisation.
