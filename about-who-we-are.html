About KBI Hong Kong

Who We Are

A youth-run, non-profit regional chapter of the Königsberger Bridges Institute — building Hong Kong's pipeline of emerging-technology talent since 2016.

Our Identity

A Regional Chapter
with Global Connections

KBI Hong Kong is the Hong Kong regional chapter of the Königsberger Bridges Institute — a global, non-profit organisation dedicated to rigorous, credible, and accessible emerging-technology education.

We were established in 2016 as the KBI network's first regional chapter, and remain its longest-running. Our role is to localise KBI's global mission by building programmes, partnerships, and communities specific to Hong Kong's education landscape — while connecting participants to KBI's international network of competitions, research, and professional development.

We are entirely youth-run. Our committee, operations, programmes, and community are led by university students and recent graduates — which means everything we build is built by people who are still actively living the challenges we are trying to solve.

Mission Statement

"To build Hong Kong's pipeline of emerging technology talent, research, and competitive excellence — connecting students, educators, institutions, and industry through programmes, competitions, and applied innovation."

What Makes Us Different
No hype, no theatre

We reject speculative, commercially driven framing. Our programmes are grounded in academic rigour and real-world application.

Student-led, genuinely

Not a corporate "youth programme". Our directors, officers, and leads are students and recent graduates making real decisions.

Global pipeline, local roots

As part of KBI's international network, participants connect to global competitions, mentors, and pathways — while remaining grounded in HK's ecosystem.

Our Values

The Principles That
Guide Everything We Do

Every programme, event, and community interaction at KBI Hong Kong is shaped by six core values. These are not aspirational posters — they are the actual criteria by which we evaluate our decisions and our work.

01

Rigour

Intellectual depth and academic seriousness in everything we design. We believe easy programmes produce easy outcomes. We choose hard.

02

Curiosity

Open-minded inquiry and continuous learning. We design programmes that teach students to ask better questions, not just give better answers.

03

Integrity

Honesty, transparency, and principled conduct — in how we run programmes, award results, engage sponsors, and communicate publicly.

04

Practicality

Applied, real-world impact over abstraction. Theory matters — but only when it connects to the problems students will actually face in their careers and research.

05

Inclusion

Open access and diverse participation across schools, universities, disciplines, and backgrounds. Excellence does not come from one type of student.

06

Collaboration

Partnership with universities, secondary schools, employers, and the technology community — because no single organisation can build an ecosystem alone.

Global Context

Part of the Königsberger
Bridges Institute

The Königsberger Bridges Institute (KBI) is an international non-profit organisation with chapters across the globe. Each chapter is responsible for localising KBI's mission in their region — adapting global frameworks to local education ecosystems while connecting participants to international opportunities.

KBI Hong Kong was the first chapter established, in 2016. As of 2026, the KBI network has grown to include chapters across Asia, Europe, and North America — all sharing competition frameworks, training materials, and research agendas that KBI HK helped shape from the beginning.
