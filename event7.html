KBI HK Annual
Community Gathering 2025

An evening of alumni networking, lightning talks from the KBI community, and a look back at what the chapter accomplished in 2025 — and what's ahead for 2026.

14 November 2025
The Hive Kennedy Town
120 attendees · 5 lightning talks
Event Recap

The Year in Review

The KBI HK Annual Community Gathering brought together 120 members, alumni, students, and supporters for an evening at The Hive Kennedy Town. The format was deliberately informal: drinks, canapés, five lightning talks from community members, and open time for the kind of conversations that don't happen in workshops or competition halls.

Chapter Director Grace Wong opened the evening with a summary of KBI HK's 2025 milestones: the largest ETO Hong Kong finals to date, the launch of the Schoolers Stream across all three competition tracks, the first ISFRETIC certification cohort, and the expansion of the Socratic Circle programme to three secondary schools.

The gathering also served as the informal launch platform for ETO Hong Kong 2026 — Grace gave the audience the first look at the expanded competition format, new collaborative project category, and enhanced Schoolers Stream support before the official announcement the following week.

Highlights from the Evening

01
What I Wish I Knew Before Competing in IBCOL
Arjun Sharma · KBI HK Alumni, IBCOL 2022 Finalist
A candid account of mistakes made and lessons learned in the competition circuit — from team dynamics to the difference between a technically correct proposal and a compelling one.
02
From IDSOL to Industry: A Data Career Path
Alumni Speaker · Data Scientist, Regional Bank
How competing in KBI HK's data science track led directly to internship opportunities, and the specific skills from IDSOL that employers actually ask about in technical interviews.
03
Teaching Blockchain to 12-Year-Olds: What Works
Socratic Circle Educator · Participating Secondary School
A teacher's first-hand account of running the Socratic Circle blockchain curriculum with Form 1 students — what engaged them, what confused them, and what surprised everyone.
04
Quantum Computing in 2025: State of Play
Raymond Cheung · HKU Quantum Research Group
A rapid-fire update on where quantum hardware actually stands, which milestones matter, and what students preparing for IQCOL 2026 should be paying attention to.
05
Preview: ETO Hong Kong 2026
Grace Wong · KBI HK Chapter Director
First public preview of ETO HK 2026 — expanded tracks, new collaborative project category, Schoolers Stream enhancements, and the opening of registration.
