About the Projects Programme

Beyond Competitions —
Building and Publishing

Competitions teach you to analyse and solve under pressure. Projects teach you to build, iterate, collaborate across months, and produce something that exists beyond a competition round. The KBI HK Projects programme is for community members who want to go further.

Each project is affiliated with a university research group or an industry partner, has a defined scope and timeline, and aims toward a concrete output — whether that is a working prototype, a published paper, an open-source tool, or a public dataset. Projects run for one or two academic terms with a small team of 2–5 participants.

Participation in a KBI HK project is selectively offered to members who have demonstrated commitment through competition or community involvement. If you have not yet been approached but are interested, register your interest below and we will be in touch when a suitable project becomes available.

Real outputs, not just participation

Projects are oriented toward concrete, public outputs — publications, tools, datasets, or working systems that add something to the world.

Mentored by researchers and practitioners

Each project has a faculty or industry advisor who provides direction, reviews outputs, and acts as a co-author or technical lead where appropriate.

Research Bursary eligible

Approved project teams may apply for the KBI HK Research Bursary to cover software, compute, or publication costs. See the scholarships page.

🔗 Blockchain
DeFi Transparency & On-Chain Governance Analysis
Started Q3 2024 · Ongoing
In collaboration with HKU DeFi Lab
● Recruiting 1 more member

An analysis of transparency mechanisms, governance token distributions, and voting participation patterns across major DeFi protocols deployed on Ethereum and alternative L1 chains. The project aims to produce a publicly available dataset and a peer-reviewed paper examining the gap between stated governance decentralisation and actual voting concentration.

Current team is 3 members (2 Computer Science, 1 Economics). Seeking one additional member with Python data analysis or smart contract reading experience.

📊 Data Science
Hong Kong Public Transit Demand Forecasting
Started January 2025 · Ongoing
In collaboration with HKUST Transportation Lab
● Full team — interest list open

A machine learning project using publicly available MTR ridership data, weather records, and event calendars to build short-term demand forecasting models for Hong Kong's Mass Transit Railway. The project combines time-series modelling with spatial analysis, and targets a working open-source forecasting tool as its primary output.

The current team of 4 is full, but we are building a waitlist for the next cohort of this project (expected Q3 2026). Register your interest now to be notified.

⚛ Quantum Computing
Quantum Error Correction Benchmarking for NISQ Devices
Starting Q3 2026 · Recruiting now
In collaboration with HKUST Quantum Computing Group
● Recruiting 3–4 members

A benchmarking study comparing the performance of leading quantum error correction protocols (including surface codes and repetition codes) on current NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) hardware via IBM Quantum and IonQ cloud access. The project aims to contribute a reproducible benchmarking methodology to the quantum computing research community.

This project is suitable for participants with a background in physics, mathematics, or computer science with some exposure to quantum computing fundamentals. Prior IQCOL HK participation is an advantage but not required.

Register Your Interest

If you are interested in participating in a KBI HK project — either one listed above or a future project — register your interest using the form below. We will contact you when a project matching your background and interests is available or recruiting.

Registration does not guarantee placement on a specific project. Placement decisions are made by the project lead and faculty advisor based on skills, availability, and team composition needs.

Prerequisite: You should be an active KBI HK member, or currently in the process of joining, to be considered for project placement. Projects are a member benefit, not a standalone programme.
