Alumni Success Stories

Where Our Alumni
Go — and What They Build

The real measure of KBI HK's impact is not attendance counts. It is what happens after people leave our programmes. These are some of the stories our alumni have shared with us.

Featured Story
Chloe Yeung
Blockchain Engineer · Major DeFi Protocol, Singapore
"I came to IBCOL HK as a second-year CS student who had read about Ethereum but never written a line of Solidity. Placing second that year was less important than the two months of preparation — it forced me to actually build something and defend how it worked in front of people who knew more than I did. That experience is what I put on my CV, and it's what got me my first interview."

IBCOL HK 2021 (2nd Place, University Track) · ISFRETIC Certified · Former KBI HK Communications Officer. Now building smart contract infrastructure at a top-tier DeFi protocol. Her team's IBCOL project became the basis for her undergraduate thesis, which was subsequently published in a blockchain conference proceedings.

Marcus Chen
Quantitative Researcher · Goldman Sachs HK

IDSOL gave me my first real encounter with messy, real-world data that didn't behave the way textbooks promised. Working through that — under competition pressure, with people watching — was the closest thing to an actual job I experienced in university.

IDSOL HK 2022 (1st Place) · HKUST Data Science — graduated 2023

Data Science Finance
IDSOL 2022 win → Offered quantitative research internship by panel judge. Converted to full-time role on graduation.
Priya Nair
PhD Candidate · ETH Zürich (Quantum Systems)

IQCOL HK introduced me to quantum computing before my university even had a course on it. The Quantum Primer Workshop got me far enough to be dangerous — and the competition got me to actually apply what I'd learned. My doctoral research grew directly from questions I first asked preparing for IQCOL 2022.

IQCOL HK 2022 · Quantum Primer Workshop 2021 · CUHK Physics

Quantum Computing Research
ETH Zürich PhD offer → Cited KBI HK IQCOL experience in personal statement. Funded research in quantum error correction.
Jason Lau
Co-Founder · Web3 Infrastructure Startup, Hong Kong

My co-founder and I met at IBCOL 2019. We had different technical strengths — I did blockchain, he did product — and the competition forced us to figure out how to work together under pressure. We raised our seed round four years later. We still reference the IBCOL problem sets when onboarding new engineers.

IBCOL HK 2019 · International Finals Qualifier · HKU Computer Science

Blockchain Entrepreneurship
KBI HK connection → Met co-founder at IBCOL 2019. Startup now employs 14 people and serves 3 enterprise clients.
Sophie Ho
Policy Analyst · HKMA FinTech Innovation Hub

I wasn't a typical KBI HK participant — I'm a law student, not an engineer. But the IDSOL challenge required a team, and mine needed someone who understood regulation. Working through real datasets with engineers taught me how to ask better technical questions. That's the skill that actually got me my job.

IDSOL HK 2023 · KBI HK Outreach Officer · HKU Law

Data Science Policy
Non-traditional background → First law graduate to serve as KBI HK Officer. Now advising on CBDC and DeFi regulatory frameworks.
Amy Tsang
Machine Learning Engineer · Google DeepMind, London

I joined KBI HK as a secondary school student who could barely explain what a neural network was. IDSOL gave me a reason to learn Python properly — not just from tutorials, but with a goal that actually mattered. Six years later, I'm building production ML systems at DeepMind. KBI HK was the beginning of that path.

IDSOL HK 2018 (Secondary School Track) · HKU BSc → UCL MSc (Distinction)

Data Science Machine Learning
Secondary school entry → One of the first secondary students to qualify at IDSOL. Now building foundation model evaluation infrastructure.
Kevin Pang
Research Scientist · IBM Quantum, Tokyo

IQCOL HK 2021 was the first quantum competition I ever entered — and the first time I had to explain quantum entanglement to a panel of non-physicists. That communication challenge turned out to be as important as the technical one. IBM hired me partly because I could translate quantum concepts for engineering and product teams.

IQCOL HK 2021 (1st Place, Inaugural Edition) · HKUST Physics PhD

Quantum Computing Research
IQCOL inaugural winner → Published two papers citing IQCOL research as preliminary work. IBM Quantum researcher since 2024.
Alumni Outcomes

Pathways Our Alumni
Take From Here

Beyond individual stories, the patterns across our alumni cohort show what kinds of outcomes KBI HK participation consistently produces — and what sectors and institutions our community members move into.

Meet the Community →

Graduate Research & Academia

Former participants now in postgraduate programmes at HKU, HKUST, CUHK, UCL, and ETH Zürich — pursuing work in blockchain systems, data science, and quantum computing.

Technology Industry Roles

Alumni working in blockchain development, data engineering, quantitative research, and ML engineering at investment banks, technology firms, and fintech startups globally.

Founding & Entrepreneurship

Multiple KBI HK alumni have co-founded technology companies — in DeFi, data infrastructure, and applied AI — citing KBI competition experience and connections as formative.

Policy & Public Sector

Alumni contributing to Hong Kong's emerging-technology policy through roles at HKMA, regulatory bodies, government innovation offices, and public-sector technology advisory groups.
