About This Workshop

What to Expect

Quantum computing is no longer a theoretical curiosity — it is rapidly approaching the threshold at which it will disrupt cryptography, optimisation, and machine learning at scale. This primer workshop gives students the conceptual foundations to engage meaningfully with the field, whether through the IQCOL competition, university coursework, or independent research.

Designed for students at ISCED levels 4–8, the workshop assumes no prior exposure to quantum mechanics or advanced mathematics. The approach is intuitive-first: we build understanding through analogies, interactive demonstrations, and structured discussion before introducing the underlying formalism. Participants leave with a coherent mental model — not just a list of buzzwords.

The afternoon session shifts from theory to application, examining real-world use cases in post-quantum cryptography, pharmaceutical research, and logistics optimisation. We'll critically evaluate the current state of quantum hardware and discuss realistic timelines — separating genuine progress from hype.

Learning Outcomes

What You'll Learn

Qubits & Superposition

How quantum bits differ from classical bits, and what superposition actually means at an intuitive level.

Entanglement

What quantum entanglement is, what it isn't, and why it is the resource that makes quantum computing powerful.

Quantum Gates & Circuits

The building blocks of quantum algorithms — how gates manipulate qubits and how circuits are composed.

Key Quantum Algorithms

Shor's algorithm, Grover's search, and VQE — why they matter and what advantage they offer over classical methods.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

How quantum computers threaten current encryption standards, and what the transition to PQC looks like in practice.

IQCOL Competition Context

How these concepts map to IQCOL judging criteria and what competition-ready quantum research looks like.

Who's Running the Session

Raymond Cheung
Quantum Computing Researcher · HKU Department of Physics
Raymond's research focuses on quantum error correction and near-term quantum algorithms. He has presented at international quantum computing conferences and serves as IQCOL's lead academic adviser for Hong Kong.

Quantum Software Engineer · IBM Quantum Network Partner
Grace builds quantum circuits using Qiskit for a Hong Kong-based IBM Quantum Network partner. A KBI HK alumni, she mentors IQCOL teams and bridges the gap between academic theory and industry application.
