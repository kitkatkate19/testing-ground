About ETO HK

What is the Emerging Technologies Olympiad?

The Emerging Technologies Olympiad (ETO) is KBI Hong Kong's flagship annual programme — a city-wide competition that serves as Hong Kong's qualifying pathway to three prestigious international olympiads.

Unlike traditional academic competitions, ETO is team-based and project-driven. Participants research, develop, and present applied work in their chosen technology track. The best teams advance to represent Hong Kong at the international stage.

"The most credible platform for students who want to genuinely understand blockchain, data science, and quantum computing — and prove it on a global stage."

— KBI HK Alumni

Competition Tracks

Three Tracks. One Olympiad.

Each technology track is aligned to an international olympiad. Teams choose one track and develop a project over the competition cycle.

Blockchain & Trust Technology
IBCOL Track — International Blockchain Olympiad

Design and present a real-world blockchain application addressing a societal or commercial challenge. Projects are judged on technical soundness, innovation, feasibility, and presentation quality.

Team-based, 2–5 members
Written paper + live presentation
Top team → IBCOL International Finals
Full competition rules
Data Science & AI
IDSOL Track — International Data Science Olympiad

Develop a data-driven research project or AI application that addresses a real-world problem. Projects must demonstrate analytical rigour, methodological clarity, and actionable insights.

Team-based, 2–5 members
Research paper + dataset + demo
Top team → IDSOL International Finals
Full competition rules
Quantum Computing
IQCOL Track — International Quantum Computing Olympiad

Investigate a quantum computing algorithm, application, or conceptual framework. Projects must demonstrate understanding of quantum principles and their potential real-world relevance.

Team-based, 2–5 members
Technical paper + presentation
Top team → IQCOL International Finals
Full competition rules

Two Streams for Every Level

Team Types

How Teams Are Formed

Schoolers Team

For School Students

A team formed entirely of Schoolers Stream participants (ISCED 1–3). These teams compete exclusively within the Schoolers Stream.

All members must be enrolled in primary or secondary education
2–5 members per team
Produces a Schoolers Project
Competes in the Schoolers Stream only
Students Team

For Tertiary Students

A team where the majority are tertiary-level students (ISCED 4–8). May include Schoolers as minority members.

Majority must be in tertiary or postgraduate education
2–5 members; mixed membership allowed
Produces a Students Project
Competes in the Students Stream
