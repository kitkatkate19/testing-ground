IBCOL HK 2024
National Qualifying Round

The qualifying round for the inaugural ETO Hong Kong — 35 teams, one day, six finalist spots. A snapshot of where blockchain education stood in Hong Kong at the start of 2024.

16 March 2024
PolyU, Hung Hom
35 teams · 6 finalists selected
Event Recap

The Inaugural Qualifying Round

IBCOL HK 2024's qualifying round was the first competitive blockchain event KBI HK had run at this scale. Thirty-five teams — drawn from universities across Hong Kong, with a handful of secondary school participants — submitted project proposals and presented before a panel of judges at PolyU's Hung Hom campus.

The judging panel included academics from HKUST and CUHK, a fintech practitioner from a Hong Kong-licensed virtual bank, and a representative from the IBCOL international secretariat. The bar was intentionally high: KBI HK wanted to signal from the outset that this was not a participation-ribbon competition.

Six teams advanced. The remaining 29 received written feedback from the panel — many cited that feedback as the most actionable input they'd received on their blockchain projects in any academic context.

What We Learned

Key Observations from the Panel

The "blockchain for everything" problem

Nearly a third of submissions proposed blockchain solutions for problems that did not require a distributed ledger. The judging panel's most common note: "Why does this need to be on blockchain?" — a question teams need to answer before they enter the room.

Technical depth varied dramatically

The gap between the strongest and weakest technical submissions was wider than expected. The top six teams demonstrated genuine understanding of consensus mechanisms, gas costs, and smart contract limitations. Others had not progressed beyond buzzword-level familiarity.

Secondary school participants outperformed expectations

Two secondary school teams submitted proposals that made it to the final selection shortlist. Though neither ultimately advanced, the panel noted their work as exceptional given their level — prompting KBI HK to formalise the Schoolers Stream for 2025.

What Changed Next

The Schoolers Stream Begins

The 2024 qualifying round's surprise finding — that secondary school students could compete at a meaningful level — directly led to the creation of the Schoolers Stream in 2025. IBCOL HK now runs two parallel streams, with dedicated judging criteria calibrated to each ISCED level range.
