Frequently Asked
Questions

Answers to common questions about KBI HK membership, competitions, certifications, events, and how to get involved.

Membership
KBI HK membership is open to secondary school students, university undergraduates, postgraduate students, and recent graduates (within 3 years of graduation) based in Hong Kong. We welcome participants from all academic disciplines — you do not need a computer science background to join or participate in our programmes.
KBI HK membership currently has a nominal annual fee that covers administrative costs, event access, and community resources. The exact fee and payment details are provided during the membership sign-up process. We also offer fee waivers for participants who demonstrate financial need — please contact us if this is relevant to you.
Members receive:
  • Priority access to competition registration (IBCOL, IDSOL, IQCOL, ETO HK)
  • Free or discounted access to workshops, clinics, and training sessions
  • Invitations to community events, networking sessions, and the annual gathering
  • Access to our members-only communication channels and resource library
  • Eligibility to apply for the APO programme and volunteer officer roles
  • Eligibility to apply for KBI HK scholarship support
Most KBI HK competitions require at least one team member to hold active KBI HK membership. In practice, we encourage all team members to join — membership is how we build a sustained community, not just a one-time event audience. Community events and public workshops are generally open to non-members.
Competitions
KBI HK runs three annual olympiad-style competitions, plus the flagship ETO HK programme:
  • IBCOL HK — International Blockchain Olympiad Hong Kong (blockchain technology, smart contracts, DeFi)
  • IDSOL HK — International Data Science Olympiad Hong Kong (real-world data analysis, statistical modelling)
  • IQCOL HK — International Quantum Computing Olympiad Hong Kong (quantum circuits, algorithms, error correction)
  • ETO HK — Emerging Technology Olympiad Hong Kong (flagship cross-track programme)
See the competitions page for current registration status.
Yes — our competitions are academically rigorous and designed to be challenging. However, "prior knowledge" does not mean a degree in the field. We provide preparatory workshops and resources (including the Quantum Primer Workshop series for IQCOL and training sessions for IBCOL). Motivated students who engage with our training resources have consistently performed well, regardless of their academic background. The ability to learn fast, think analytically, and work in teams matters more than pre-existing expertise.
Teams are self-formed and typically consist of 2–4 members. You are responsible for forming your own team prior to registration. We do facilitate a team-matching process for individuals looking for teammates — ask in the members' community channel or at a community event. Mixed teams (combining secondary and university students, or students from different disciplines) are permitted and often produce strong results.
Yes. Top-placing teams in IBCOL HK and IDSOL HK are eligible to qualify for international KBI finals stages, which have historically been held in Europe and North America. KBI HK teams have participated in and placed at international finals multiple times. Travel and accommodation support for qualifying teams is subject to available funding — contact us for details specific to each cycle.
The Emerging Technology Olympiad (ETO HK) is KBI HK's flagship programme — a cross-track challenge that combines elements of blockchain, data science, and quantum computing into a single integrated competition. It is designed for more advanced participants who have some experience in at least one of the three tracks. ETO HK is held annually and represents the highest-level competition KBI HK offers. See the ETO HK 2026 page for this year's programme.
Certifications & Training
The ISFRETIC (International Standard Framework for Rigorous Emerging Technology Instruction and Certification) is KBI's internationally recognised certification framework. It covers foundational and advanced competencies in blockchain, data science, and quantum computing. KBI HK delivers ISFRETIC training locally, and participants who pass the assessment receive certification recognised across the KBI global network. See the certifications page for current intake information.
ISFRETIC certification is recognised by KBI's global network of partner organisations and is valued as a signal of rigorous, structured learning in emerging technologies. Recognition by specific employers varies — we recommend checking with your target employer and noting that ISFRETIC certification is most meaningful when combined with competition participation, project experience, and demonstrated practical skills.
No — ISFRETIC training and KBI HK competitions are parallel pathways. You can enter competitions without completing ISFRETIC training, and you can pursue certification without entering competitions. That said, many participants find that the training curriculum provides useful preparation for the competition environment, and we recommend engaging with both if time permits.
Events & Workshops
Many KBI HK community events and workshops are open to the public, while competition-specific events and member-only gatherings require membership. Check the events page for the registration status and access requirements for each individual event. When in doubt, register your interest and we will confirm eligibility.
Upcoming events are listed on the events page and announced through our member newsletter, social media channels, and community communication platforms. Members typically receive event announcements 2–4 weeks in advance. Signing up for membership is the most reliable way to stay informed.
KBI HK runs a mix of in-person, online, and hybrid events depending on the format and scale. Competition days, community gatherings, and major workshops are typically in-person in Hong Kong. Some preparatory training sessions and smaller workshops may be online or hybrid. Each event listing specifies the format clearly.
Volunteering & Team Roles
The standard pathway is through the Associate Programme Officer (APO) programme, which is open to current KBI HK members. APO intake is announced twice per year through member channels. After completing an APO cycle, you can apply for a full Programme Officer role. See the Our Team page for a full explanation of the structure and how to apply.
Yes. KBI HK occasionally recruits event volunteers on an ad hoc basis for larger events such as competition days and the annual gathering. Volunteer calls are posted through the members' channel. Event volunteering is a great way to get a feel for how KBI HK operates and to meet the team before considering a more formal APO application.
Yes — we actively welcome programme and event proposals from community members. The best way to submit a proposal is through our contact form, clearly outlining the idea, target audience, and your suggested involvement. Proposals from current members are reviewed by the committee at our monthly planning meetings.
Sponsors & Partners
We work with sponsors and partners across several categories — programme sponsors, event partners, prize donors, and institution collaborators. See the sponsors page for an overview of partnership tiers, or fill in the sponsor enquiry form and our Sponsorship Officer will be in touch within 5 working days.
Absolutely. KBI HK works with secondary schools, universities, and research institutes across Hong Kong. Institutional collaboration can take the form of campus ambassador programmes, joint event co-organisation, research project partnerships, or formal MOU-based agreements. Contact us through the collaborations page or reach out directly via our contact form.
