Industry Panel:
Blockchain in Financial Services

A practitioner-led panel bringing together blockchain engineers, fintech executives, and regulatory advisers to discuss the real state of blockchain deployment in Hong Kong's financial sector — no hype, just honest assessment.

18 September 2024
HKMA Fintech Supervisory Sandbox, Central
5 panelists · 85 attendees
Event Recap

What the Panel Discussed

The Industry Panel on Blockchain in Financial Services was KBI HK's first explicitly industry-facing event — designed not for competition preparation, but to give students and early-career professionals unfiltered access to practitioners who work with blockchain technology every day in Hong Kong's financial sector.

Five panelists — drawn from a virtual asset exchange, a licensed bank's digital asset custody unit, a regulatory consultancy, a Hong Kong-listed fintech company, and HKUST's Centre for Blockchain Research — spent 90 minutes in structured discussion moderated by KBI HK's academic adviser, followed by 45 minutes of open audience Q&A.

The format was deliberately candid. Panelists were asked to describe what blockchain actually does (and doesn't do) in their organisations — not what it could theoretically do. The result was one of the most honest conversations about blockchain in Hong Kong finance that the KBI community had encountered.

Who Was on the Panel

Arjun Sharma
Blockchain Engineer · Regional Fintech Firm
Discussed the practical challenges of deploying smart contracts in a regulated financial environment — gas cost management, upgrade patterns, and the gap between what Solidity can do and what compliance teams will allow.
Dr. Michelle Lam
Research Fellow · HKUST Centre for Blockchain Research
Provided the academic perspective — where the research frontier is, what the most promising near-term applications are, and why the gap between research and production deployment remains wider than the press coverage suggests.
Grace Wong
Head of Digital Assets · Licensed Virtual Bank
Spoke candidly about the regulatory realities of operating a digital asset custody business under the HKMA's licensing framework — what Project Ensemble changed, what it didn't, and what she wished students understood before applying to work in the sector.
Key Themes

What the Audience Took Away

"The most useful thing I've heard in two years of studying fintech: blockchain solves the problem of parties who don't trust each other needing to share data. If you have trust, you probably don't need it." — Audience member, postgraduate finance student

The panel's recurring theme: the highest-value blockchain applications in Hong Kong finance are in settlement (tokenised assets, cross-border payment rails) and provenance (trade finance, supply chain attestation) — areas where the immutability and transparency properties are genuinely useful, not just fashionable.
