KBI HK Alumni
Reunion & Networking Night

Eight years of KBI Hong Kong, gathered in one room. The 2023 alumni reunion brought together graduates spanning every cohort from the chapter's founding — from 2015 through to the Class of 2023.

8 December 2023
The Landmark Mandarin Oriental, Central
95 alumni · 8 cohort years represented
Event Recap

Eight Years, One Room

The 2023 KBI HK Alumni Reunion was the chapter's largest gathering to that point — 95 alumni from across eight cohort years, spanning careers in fintech, research, consulting, policy, and education. Some attendees had not been in the same room since competing together a decade earlier.

The evening was deliberately unstructured. No panels, no formal presentations beyond a brief address from Chapter Director Grace Wong and a recognition segment for the chapter's longest-serving alumni advisers. The primary purpose was connection — reminding a community that had grown significantly of what they share.

The venue — the Landmark Mandarin Oriental's private event space — was a deliberate choice. KBI HK had spent years operating in university seminar rooms and co-working spaces. The 2023 reunion signalled a maturation: this is a community that has built something worth celebrating with appropriate formality.

Alumni Spotlights

Where Are They Now

Dr. Michelle Lam
KBI HK Class of 2018 · IBCOL Finalist
Now Research Fellow in Distributed Systems at HKUST. Has published 14 peer-reviewed papers on consensus protocols and serves as a judge at IBCOL international finals. Returned as a keynote speaker at ETO HK 2025.
Arjun Sharma
KBI HK Class of 2020 · IBCOL Finalist
Blockchain Engineer at a leading Hong Kong fintech firm. Actively mentors current IBCOL teams and ran the 2023 Smart Contract Developer Workshop. One of KBI HK's most engaged alumni volunteers.
Grace Wong
KBI HK Class of 2016 · Chapter Director
Led the chapter from 2021 following the operational restructure. Under her direction, KBI HK launched ETO Hong Kong, expanded to three competition tracks, and grew membership by over 300%. She gave the keynote address at the reunion.
Community

Stay Connected

The KBI HK alumni network continues to grow. Current students become alumni every year, and the community remains the chapter's strongest asset. Whether you competed a decade ago or graduated last semester, the alumni network is open to all former participants.

"What surprised me most was how many people had built careers that touched what we were doing as students. It doesn't feel like a club anymore. It feels like a genuine professional network." — Alumni attendee, Class of 2019
