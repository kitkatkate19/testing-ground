Est. 2016 · Founding Story

A Bridge Built on a Simple Belief

KBI Hong Kong was established in September 2016 as the first Hong Kong chapter of the Königsberger Bridges Institute — a global network of student-led chapters advancing rigorous academic engagement with emerging technology. The founding team of seven students at HKU shared one conviction: that Hong Kong's students deserved a serious, academically grounded space to engage with blockchain technology beyond the noise of market speculation. The chapter ran its first public event in November 2016 and has operated continuously since.

KBI Hong Kong — Chapter Launch & Inaugural Gathering

The founding event of KBI Hong Kong — an informal evening gathering in a seminar room at HKU's Centennial Campus where the seven founding members presented their vision to a first cohort of 38 curious students. The agenda was simple: explain what blockchain actually is, explain what KBI Hong Kong would do, and invite students to join the founding cohort. The evening ended with more questions than answers — which, in retrospect, was exactly right.

Inaugural Public Lecture: "What Is Blockchain, and Why Does It Matter?"

The chapter's first public-facing event — an 80-minute lecture open to the entire HKU student body, covering the mechanics of distributed ledgers, the origins of Bitcoin, and the emerging use of smart contracts on Ethereum. Delivered by a founding member with support from a visiting speaker from the KBI global network, the lecture attracted 95 attendees — nearly three times what the organisers had expected. A waiting list had to be managed for the first time. The event set the tone for everything KBI Hong Kong would build over the following decade.
