IBCOL Hong Kong 2026 —
Call for Participants Now Open

KBI Hong Kong Editorial
Official KBI HK Announcement
15 January 2026
4 min read

KBI Hong Kong is pleased to announce that the International Blockchain Olympiad Hong Kong 2026 (IBCOL HK 2026) qualifying round is now open for participant registration. IBCOL HK is Hong Kong's official qualifying pathway for the International Blockchain Olympiad — the world's premier blockchain competition for students.

Teams that place first in their respective streams at the IBCOL HK qualifying round will represent Hong Kong at the IBCOL international finals. Hong Kong has participated in IBCOL since 2022 and has placed in the top three internationally in two of the four years since.

"IBCOL is not a coding competition. It is a challenge to build something that matters — a blockchain application with a real use case, argued with rigour and presented with clarity."

— KBI Hong Kong IBCOL Track Director

What Is IBCOL?

The International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL) challenges student teams to design and present a blockchain-based solution to a real-world problem. Teams produce a written project paper — typically 15–25 pages — and deliver a live presentation to an expert judging panel. The emphasis is on rigour of reasoning, practical applicability, and technical depth: teams must demonstrate not just that blockchain is technically feasible for their chosen problem, but that it is the most appropriate solution compared to alternatives.

Topics in previous years have ranged from supply chain provenance and healthcare records to carbon credit trading, financial inclusion, and digital identity. The breadth of problem domains means that teams from diverse academic backgrounds — computer science, business, law, engineering, social science — can all contribute meaningfully.

The 2026 Competition Format

IBCOL HK 2026 follows the two-stream ETO HK model introduced for the full programme cycle:

  • Schoolers Stream (ISCED 1–3) — Open to primary and secondary school students. Schoolers Teams are judged within their own stream against age-appropriate benchmarks.
  • Students Stream (ISCED 4–8) — Open to sub-degree, undergraduate, and postgraduate students. Mixed teams with a tertiary majority may include secondary school members.

Both streams compete at the same qualifying event, with separate judging panels and prize categories. The top team from each stream advances to the IBCOL international finals.

IBCOL HK 2026 — Key Details
Team size: 2–5 members; multi-school and multi-institution teams welcome
Format: Written project paper (15–25 pages) + live presentation (15 min) + Q&A (10 min)
Two streams: Schoolers (ISCED 1–3) and Students (ISCED 4–8)
Top team per stream represents Hong Kong at IBCOL international finals
Qualifying round date: Q2 2026 (exact date to be confirmed with registered teams)
Preparatory clinic: Blockchain Foundations Clinic, July 2026 (open to all registrants)

Who Should Apply

IBCOL is designed for students who want to engage seriously with a real-world problem — not students who want to learn blockchain in a classroom setting. The competition rewards depth of thinking, quality of argument, and practical sensibility. Technical blockchain implementation is part of the project, but it is not the primary criterion: a project with a genuinely compelling use case and sound reasoning will score above a technically impressive project with a weak problem rationale.

Strong IBCOL candidates typically have one or more of the following characteristics:

  • Genuine curiosity about a specific real-world problem that blockchain could address
  • Academic background in computer science, information systems, finance, law, or related fields
  • Experience with academic writing, research methodology, or structured argumentation
  • Interest in emerging technology policy, governance, or economics — not just implementation
  • Appetite for the full project lifecycle: problem framing, research, design, writing, and live presentation

No prior blockchain development experience is required. KBI HK's Blockchain Foundations Clinic is specifically designed to give new participants the conceptual foundation they need to compete effectively.

IBCOL HK's Track Record

Since KBI Hong Kong became an official IBCOL chapter in 2022, the programme has grown from 22 teams in the inaugural qualifying round to 42 teams in 2025. Hong Kong teams have consistently performed well internationally: in 2024, the Hong Kong Students Stream champion placed second at the IBCOL international finals — the chapter's highest international placing to date.

The 2025 qualifying round marked the first year IBCOL HK formally adopted the Schoolers Stream. Three secondary school teams competed in 2025 — a number the chapter expects to grow substantially in 2026, as awareness of the programme spreads through the secondary school network.

"The teams that win IBCOL HK do not just know blockchain. They know why blockchain — why it is the right tool for the problem they have chosen, and why the alternatives fall short."

— Dr. Michelle Lam, KBI HK Chapter Director
