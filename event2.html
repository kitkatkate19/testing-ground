About This Event

Hong Kong's Emerging Tech Community, Together

The KBI Community Meet-Up is a quarterly tradition — a relaxed, informal evening where the diverse threads of Hong Kong's emerging technology community come together in one room.

Expect five short lightning talks from community members (students, practitioners, alumni, and researchers), followed by extended open networking with drinks and light food. There's no agenda beyond connection and curiosity.

Whether you're a secondary school student taking your first steps into blockchain, a postdoc doing quantum computing research, or an industry professional looking to reconnect with the academic world — you belong here.

"The KBI meet-ups are where I've found every co-founder, mentor, and collaborator I've ever worked with. Worth every minute."

— KBI HK Alumni, now Fintech Founder
This Edition

Lightning Talks — March 2026

Five 8-minute talks from community members. Provocative, practical, and always open to debate.

01
Is DeFi Actually Solving Anything? A Sceptic's View
Arjun Sharma · Blockchain Engineer
A practitioner's honest critique of decentralised finance — what's working, what's vaporware, and where students should focus their research energy.
02
How I Went from IBCOL to a Research Fellowship in 18 Months
Dr. Michelle Lam · HKUST Research Fellow
A personal trajectory talk from one of KBI HK's most prominent alumni — and how the competition experience shaped her research agenda.
03
Quantum Advantage: What It Actually Means and When We'll See It
Raymond Cheung · PhD Candidate, HKU Physics
Cutting through the noise on quantum computing timelines — a grounded, academic perspective on what "quantum advantage" means in practice.
04
Our IDSOL Project: From Dataset to Finalist
HKUST IDSOL 2025 Team — Students Stream
The 2025 HKUST IDSOL finalist team walks through their project — what worked, what didn't, and the one change that got them to the international stage.
05
Why Schools Are Finally Taking Blockchain Seriously
Grace Wong · Head of STEM, DSS Secondary School
An educator's perspective on the growing uptake of blockchain and AI content in Hong Kong secondary curricula — and where KBI fits in.
