New Collaborations

What We Look For
in a Collaboration

We are selective about partnerships. The right collaborator contributes substantive expertise that genuinely improves what our students experience — not just a logo on a page.

🎯

Substantive Contribution

We are looking for partners who contribute real expertise — as judges, curriculum advisors, dataset providers, or research co-supervisors. Logo-only arrangements are not meaningful to us.

🔬

Technical Depth

Preferred partners have genuine depth in one of our three technology domains — blockchain, data science, or quantum computing — at an academic or applied professional level.

👥

Student Focus

The collaboration should ultimately benefit our student members — through better programme quality, access to datasets, exposure to professional-level feedback, or research opportunities.

🤝

Long-Term Relationship

We prefer collaborations that develop over time — where mutual familiarity allows us to work more effectively together and where both sides genuinely benefit from the arrangement.
