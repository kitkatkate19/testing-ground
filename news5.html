KBI HK Chapter Rebuild:
A New Operational Structure for 2026

KBI Hong Kong Executive Committee
Official Press Release
12 March 2026
5 min read

KBI Hong Kong is pleased to announce the completion of a comprehensive operational restructure, effective from the 2026 programme cycle. The restructure establishes clearer lines of responsibility across all programme areas, introduces a new tiered volunteer leadership framework, and creates dedicated committee roles for each competition track under ETO HK.

This announcement follows an internal review conducted throughout the final quarter of 2025, during which the Executive Committee assessed the chapter's operational model against the demands of its expanded programme portfolio. The review found that while KBI HK had grown significantly since its 2022 relaunch — adding IQCOL in 2024, launching the Schoolers Stream in 2025, and more than doubling its membership base — the underlying committee structure had not evolved at the same pace.

"We have built something real here. The restructure is about making sure the organisation can sustain and grow what we have built — without relying on a handful of people doing everything."

— KBI Hong Kong Chapter Director

What Has Changed

The 2026 operational structure introduces four primary changes to how KBI HK operates:

1. Dedicated Track Directors

Each of the three ETO HK competition tracks — IBCOL, IDSOL, and IQCOL — now has a dedicated Track Director. Track Directors are responsible for the end-to-end delivery of their respective qualifying rounds: judge recruitment, participant communication, marking coordination, and liaison with the relevant international olympiad organising body. Previously, these responsibilities were managed collectively by the Executive Committee, which created coordination bottlenecks as participant numbers grew.

2. A Formalised Volunteer Leadership Tier

KBI HK has historically relied on an informal pool of volunteers for event support, mentoring, and outreach. From 2026, the chapter introduces a formal Associate Programme Officer (APO) role — a structured, time-bounded volunteer position with defined responsibilities and a clear pathway to committee membership. APOs are recruited each February and serve for one full programme cycle (February through December).

The APO structure serves two purposes: it gives the chapter a reliable, accountable volunteer pipeline, and it gives early-career professionals and recent graduates a meaningful leadership development opportunity within a well-regarded academic organisation.

3. Separated Membership and Outreach Functions

The chapter's growth objectives for 2026 require substantially more proactive outreach than in previous cycles — particularly to secondary schools and to institutions outside the traditional CUHK/HKU/HKUST cluster. To support this, the Membership and Outreach functions have been separated into two distinct committee portfolios, each with its own lead and workplan.

4. A Formal Sponsorship Relations Function

Sponsorship has historically been handled on an ad hoc basis by Executive Committee members with existing industry relationships. The 2026 structure creates a dedicated Sponsorship Relations Officer role, responsible for maintaining an active sponsor pipeline, managing sponsor benefits delivery, and developing a multi-year sponsorship strategy aligned to the chapter's programme roadmap.

2026 Committee Structure at a Glance
Chapter Director — Overall strategic leadership and international KBI liaison
Deputy Director — Operations oversight, committee coordination, governance
Track Directors × 3 — IBCOL HK, IDSOL HK, IQCOL HK (one each)
Membership Officer — Member acquisition, onboarding, and retention
Outreach Officer — School and university partnerships, community engagement
Sponsorship Relations Officer — Sponsor pipeline, benefits delivery, strategy
Events Officer — Non-competition events: clinics, meet-ups, seminars
Communications Officer — Website, social media, news and publications

Committee Roles and Responsibilities

The following table summarises the primary accountabilities attached to each committee role in the 2026 structure:

Role Primary Accountabilities
Chapter Director Strategic direction; international KBI relationship; public representation; final sign-off on major decisions
Deputy Director Committee coordination; operational governance; APO programme management; Chapter Director cover
Track Director (×3) Full delivery of qualifying round for assigned track; judge recruitment; international liaision; participant support
Membership Officer Member database management; onboarding journey; renewal campaigns; member benefits delivery
Outreach Officer School and university MOU management; teacher and faculty engagement; ambassador programme
Sponsorship Relations Officer Sponsor prospecting and proposals; contract management; benefits fulfilment; multi-year strategy
Events Officer Clinic, workshop, meet-up, and seminar logistics; venue management; registration systems
Communications Officer Website content; social media channels; news articles; press releases; design asset oversight

Associate Programme Officer Programme

The APO programme is open to KBI HK members who have completed at least one full programme cycle as a participant or general volunteer. APOs are assigned to a specific committee portfolio and work directly under the guidance of the relevant committee lead.

The APO role is designed for early-career professionals and penultimate or final-year students who want substantive organisational experience in a structured, professional environment. Responsibilities are real and meaningful — APOs are not performing administrative tasks, they are contributing to the delivery of programmes that serve hundreds of participants annually.

Applications for the 2026 APO cohort will open in early February. Interested candidates should contact the KBI HK secretariat to be notified when applications open.

"The APO programme is one of the structural changes I am most excited about. It creates a talent pipeline for the chapter, and it gives our most engaged members a real leadership opportunity."

— KBI Hong Kong Deputy Director

What Stays the Same

It is worth being explicit about what the restructure does not change. The core mission of KBI HK — to develop the next generation of blockchain, data science, and quantum computing talent in Hong Kong through rigorous, internationally benchmarked competition — remains unchanged. The three-track ETO HK model, the Schoolers and Students streams, the emphasis on written research papers and live presentations, and the community-first approach to membership all continue exactly as before.

The restructure is an operational change, not a strategic one. It is designed to make the chapter more capable of executing on the mission at scale, not to change the mission itself.

Next Steps

Full committee appointments for the 2026 cycle were confirmed in February 2026. Details of committee members will be published on the About page shortly. APO applications will open in March 2026 with a rolling admissions process.

Members with questions about the restructure or interest in APO roles are welcome to reach out via the contact page.

Get Involved

Interested in joining the KBI HK committee or applying for an APO role? Get in touch — we welcome motivated individuals who want to contribute to serious tech education in Hong Kong.
