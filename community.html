Community

A Community Built
on Serious Engagement

KBI Hong Kong is more than a programme provider — it is a sustained community of students, educators, alumni, mentors, and partners who share a commitment to rigorous, non-hype engagement with blockchain, data science, and quantum computing.

Who We Are

1,000+ Members.
One Shared Standard.

The KBI Hong Kong community spans secondary school students, university undergraduates and postgraduates, educators, alumni, volunteer programme leads, industry mentors, and institutional partners. What unites this diverse group is a shared standard: intellectual rigour, genuine curiosity, and a refusal to engage with hype over substance.

This is a community where showing your working matters as much as getting the right answer — where serious effort is recognised regardless of outcome, and where long-term engagement is valued over one-time participation.

Within the Community

KBI HK's community is organised into groups that serve different functions — from competition preparation to applied research to peer study. Members can participate in multiple groups across different technology tracks.

01

Study Groups

Peer-led groups for students preparing for IBCOL, IDSOL, or IQCOL. Focus on technical foundations, problem-solving practice, and collaborative learning in a structured weekly format.

Research Groups

Technology Communities

Three Technology
Communities. One Ecosystem.

KBI HK organises its community around three technology tracks, each with its own study groups, research focus, and competition pathway. Members typically focus on one track but are encouraged to explore all three.

Blockchain

Blockchain Community

Focused on distributed ledger technology, smart contracts, and blockchain application design. The IBCOL competition pathway connects members to Hong Kong and international stages.

  • ·IBCOL study and preparation groups
  • ·DLT and smart contract fundamentals workshops
  • ·Blockchain application research projects
Explore IBCOL
Data Science

Data Science Community

Focused on statistical reasoning, machine learning fundamentals, and real-world data analysis. The IDSOL competition pathway develops the analytical rigour that serious data practice demands.

  • ·IDSOL study and preparation groups
  • ·Statistics, Python, and ML fundamentals
  • ·Applied dataset research and analysis
Explore IDSOL
Quantum Computing

Quantum Computing Community

Focused on quantum mechanics foundations, Qiskit programming, and quantum algorithm design. The IQCOL pathway offers one of the most intellectually rigorous student challenges in Hong Kong.

  • ·IQCOL study and preparation groups
  • ·Qiskit and quantum circuit fundamentals
  • ·Quantum simulation and algorithm research

Volunteer With Us

Join the KBI HK Team

KBI Hong Kong is entirely volunteer-run. Every event, competition, and community initiative is built by a small, committed team of students and recent graduates. If you want to go beyond attending programmes and actually help build them, we want to hear from you.

Open Now

Associate Programme Officer (APO)

The structured entry point to the KBI HK committee. APOs are placed with a Programme Officer function — Events, Communications, Outreach, or Membership — and take on real responsibilities with mentored guidance for one academic year. The majority of our current committee came through this route.

Learn About the APO Programme →
Experienced Members

Programme Officer

Full officer roles across seven functions: Membership, Outreach, Sponsorship, Events, Communications, Research, and Curriculum. Officers lead their function independently, manage APOs, and report to the Deputy Director. We recruit officers from our APO alumni and occasionally externally for specialist functions.

Enquire About Officer Roles →
Domain Expert

Track Director

Track Directors lead one of KBI HK's three competition programmes — IBCOL HK (Blockchain), IDSOL HK (Data Science), or IQCOL HK (Quantum). This role demands deep domain expertise and is recruited independently from the officer pathway. We currently have one open Track Director role for Data Science.

Express Interest →
Event-Based

Event Volunteer

Not ready to commit to a full officer role yet? Event volunteers support specific KBI HK programmes — competition days, workshops, community gatherings, and the annual ETO HK finals. This is how many of our current team members got started, and it counts as demonstrated engagement when applying for APO.

Sign Up to Volunteer →
Academic / Industry

Mentor or Judge

Practitioners and academics who want to contribute expertise without full committee membership. Mentors support student teams during competition preparation. Judges evaluate submissions and presentations at IBCOL, IDSOL, IQCOL, and ETO HK. Time commitment is bounded and flexible.

Register as Mentor or Judge →

Not Sure Where to Start?

If you are interested in contributing but not sure which role fits, reach out directly. We will have an honest conversation about what we need, what you can offer, and where the right starting point is. We prefer real conversations over long application forms.

What We Look For

We don't require a perfect CV or a specific degree. We look for genuine curiosity about emerging technology, demonstrated reliability, honest communication, and the willingness to do unglamorous operational work that makes programmes actually happen. KBI HK is a serious organisation — and serious organisations need people who show up consistently, not just when it's exciting.
