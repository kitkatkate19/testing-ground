IBCOL HK 2022
National Qualifying Round

The event that started everything — KBI HK's first competition as an officially recognised IBCOL national chapter. Twenty-two teams, one judging panel, and the beginning of a tradition.

12 March 2022
HKUST, Clear Water Bay
22 teams · 4 finalists advanced
Event Recap

Where It All Began

March 2022 marks the moment KBI Hong Kong became a competition organiser, not just a study group. The chapter had received official recognition as an IBCOL national chapter in late 2021, and the 2022 qualifying round was the proof of concept — could KBI HK run a credible, rigorous competition that was worthy of the IBCOL name?

Twenty-two teams registered, drawn largely from HKUST and CUHK. The format was straightforward: a written project proposal submitted two weeks in advance, followed by a 15-minute live presentation and 10-minute Q&A on the day. Four teams advanced to the IBCOL Asia-Pacific round. One reached the international finals.

The event was run by six volunteers, funded by a modest alumni donation, and held in a borrowed seminar room at HKUST. By every measure, it worked — and the chapter has been building on it ever since.

How Far We've Come

The Chapter's IBCOL Journey

2022
First official IBCOL HK Qualifying Round
22 teams · 4 finalists · 1 international finalist. Run by 6 volunteers in a borrowed seminar room at HKUST.
2023
IBCOL HK grows to 30 teams
The chapter ran its second qualifying round with a dedicated venue at HKU. Two Hong Kong teams advanced to the IBCOL Asia-Pacific finals.
2024
IBCOL integrated into ETO Hong Kong
35 teams competed in the first IBCOL qualifying round under the ETO Hong Kong umbrella. Finals integrated into ETO HK 2024 at CUHK.
2025
Schoolers Stream launches
42 teams across two streams — the inaugural Schoolers Stream (ISCED 1–3) and the established Students Stream. 6 finalists at ETO HK 2025 Finals Day.
2026
IBCOL HK 2026 — now accepting registrations
The competition continues to grow. Both Schoolers and Students streams open. Register by the deadline to secure your spot.
The Next Chapter

IBCOL HK 2026

Every competition cycle builds on what came before. The standards that were set in that borrowed HKUST seminar room in 2022 — rigour, honesty about technical claims, genuine engagement with the problem — still define what KBI HK looks for in IBCOL submissions today.
