Smart Contract
Developer Workshop

KBI HK's most technically demanding event to date — a full-day workshop taking participants from zero Solidity experience to a deployed, tested smart contract on an Ethereum testnet.

20 May 2023
Cyberport, Pok Fu Lam
40 participants · coding prerequisite required
Event Recap

A Workshop With Prerequisites

The Smart Contract Developer Workshop was KBI HK's first explicitly technical hands-on event — and the first to require prerequisites. Applicants had to demonstrate basic Python or JavaScript knowledge before being accepted. Of 112 applicants, 40 were admitted.

The decision to gate entry was deliberate. Previous workshops had suffered from the "developer gap" — attendees with varying levels of technical background made it impossible to pitch the session correctly. By requiring prerequisites, the 2023 Smart Contract Workshop could go deep, fast.

By 5 PM, every participant had deployed a tested ERC-20 token contract to the Sepolia testnet, audited a deliberately vulnerable contract, and submitted a written post-mortem on the vulnerability they found. The feedback was the highest of any KBI HK event to that point — 94% of participants rated it "excellent" or "outstanding."

Curriculum

What Was Built

Morning Session 1
Solidity Fundamentals: Types, Functions, and State
Syntax, variable types, modifiers, events, and the EVM execution model. Participants wrote and tested their first contract — a simple counter — in Remix IDE within the first 90 minutes.
Morning Session 2
ERC-20 Token Standard: Building from Scratch
Walking through the OpenZeppelin ERC-20 implementation, then building a custom token contract with mint, burn, and transfer controls. Each participant deployed their token to Sepolia before lunch.
Afternoon Session 1
Testing with Hardhat: Writing Automated Contract Tests
Introduction to Hardhat test framework, writing unit tests in JavaScript, fork testing against mainnet state. Emphasis on the testing mindset — why a contract with 100% test coverage can still be exploitable.
Afternoon Session 2
Smart Contract Security: Find the Bug
Participants received a deliberately vulnerable ERC-20 contract and had 90 minutes to identify the vulnerability (a reentrancy attack vector), write an exploit, and produce a written post-mortem. Led by Arjun Sharma.
Next Steps

IBCOL 2026 Preparation

Smart contract development skills are directly applicable to IBCOL project work. Teams that can prototype their blockchain solution — even at a basic level — consistently score higher on technical credibility during judging. The Blockchain Foundations Clinic in March 2026 is the first step for those building toward IBCOL.
