International Olympiads · Hong Kong

Three Olympiads.
One City.

KBI Hong Kong organises the official Hong Kong qualifying rounds for three international technology olympiads — open to secondary school and university students across the city.

Blockchain Olympiad

IBCOL Hong Kong

The International Blockchain Olympiad (Hong Kong qualifying round) is a rigorous annual competition for students who want to go beyond buzzwords — demonstrating real depth in distributed ledger technology, smart contract design, and real-world blockchain applications.

KBI Hong Kong organises the full qualifying and preparation pipeline for IBCOL. Teams present comprehensive research projects evaluated on technical depth, feasibility, and presentation quality by a panel of blockchain industry practitioners and academics.

What You'll Work On
⛓️

DLT Fundamentals

Blockchain architecture, consensus mechanisms, public/private ledgers, and the cryptographic foundations of distributed systems.

📜

Smart Contracts

Solidity programming, contract design patterns, security considerations, and deployment on test networks.

🔍

Use-Case Analysis

Critically evaluating real-world blockchain applications across finance, supply chain, identity, and governance sectors.

🏗️

DeFi & Web3

Decentralised finance protocols, token economics, and the broader ecosystem of Web3 applications and governance structures.

Competition Format

Team Registration

Teams of 1–3 members register through KBI HK membership. Both school and university streams welcomed.

Qualifying Round

Written problem-solving round assessing technical knowledge and analytical approach. Top teams advance.

Project Development

Advancing teams develop a full blockchain research project over 4–6 weeks with optional KBI HK clinic support.

Finals Presentation

Projects presented to an industry panel. Winners qualify to represent Hong Kong at the IBCOL international stage.

International Qualifying Pathway

Top-performing IBCOL HK teams qualify to represent Hong Kong at the IBCOL international competition. The International Blockchain Olympiad brings together top student teams from across Asia and beyond for the global final round.

Data Science Olympiad

IDSOL Hong Kong

The International Data Science Olympiad (Hong Kong qualifying round) challenges students with real datasets and real problems — requiring end-to-end pipeline construction, statistical rigour, and the kind of clear communication of findings that separates good data scientists from great ones.

KBI Hong Kong organises the qualifying rounds and preparation clinics for IDSOL. Participants work individually or in pairs to analyse datasets and present their findings and methodology to a panel of industry data scientists and academics.

What You'll Work On
🐍

Data Wrangling & EDA

Cleaning, transforming, and exploring datasets using Python, Pandas, and visualisation libraries to surface meaningful patterns.

🤖

Machine Learning

Supervised and unsupervised learning, model selection, evaluation metrics, and real-world predictive analytics projects.

📊

Statistical Communication

Rigorous interpretation and clear communication of statistical findings to non-technical audiences — a critical professional skill.

🔬

Applied Research

Real-world problem framing, hypothesis formation, methodology design, and reproducible analytical workflows.

Competition Format

Registration

Individual or pairs register via KBI HK membership. No prior competition experience required.

Qualifying Round

A structured data analysis challenge assessing methodology, interpretation, and communication. Top participants advance.

Deep Dive Project

Advancing participants tackle a more complex dataset challenge with KBI HK clinic support available throughout.

Finals Presentation

Presentations to an industry judging panel. Winners qualify for the IDSOL international stage.

International Qualifying Pathway

Top-performing IDSOL HK participants qualify to represent Hong Kong at the IDSOL international competition — competing against the best student data scientists from across the region and beyond.

Quantum Computing Olympiad

IQCOL Hong Kong

The International Quantum Computing Olympiad (Hong Kong qualifying round) is designed for students ready to engage with one of the most consequential technologies of the coming decade — building real understanding of quantum circuits, algorithms, and computational advantage.

KBI Hong Kong's IQCOL track includes problem-solving challenges using Qiskit and quantum circuit design, assessed by academics and researchers at the quantum computing frontier. No physics background is required — mathematical foundations are provided through our Quantum Primer preparation workshops.

What You'll Work On
⚛️

Quantum Fundamentals

Superposition, entanglement, quantum gates, and the circuit model — the conceptual foundations of quantum information processing.

💻

Qiskit Programming

Hands-on quantum circuit programming with IBM's Qiskit framework, running simulations and actual quantum hardware experiments.

🧮

Quantum Algorithms

Grover's search, quantum Fourier transform, variational algorithms — understanding where quantum provides genuine computational advantage.

🔬

Quantum Error Correction

Understanding noise, decoherence, and error correction approaches that determine the practical limits of current quantum hardware.

Competition Format

Registration & Primer

Participants register via KBI HK membership and are encouraged to attend the Quantum Primer Workshop preparation series.

Qualifying Round

Theoretical and circuit-based problems testing quantum computing knowledge and Qiskit proficiency. Top participants advance.

Project Challenge

Advanced participants tackle an applied quantum computing challenge — designing circuits, running experiments, and interpreting results.

Finals Presentation

Presentations assessed by quantum researchers and industry experts. Winners qualify for the IQCOL international stage.

💡

No Physics Background Required

KBI HK's Quantum Primer workshop series provides the mathematical and conceptual foundations needed to compete in IQCOL HK. We have had top performers who came in with no prior quantum knowledge.

International Qualifying Pathway

Top-performing IQCOL HK participants qualify to represent Hong Kong at the IQCOL international competition — one of the most technically demanding student competitions in quantum technology globally.

Who Can Compete

Two Streams. Both Welcome.

All three competitions recognise two contestant streams based on education level — ensuring that school students and university students are evaluated appropriately against their peers.

Schoolers Stream
ISCED Level 1–3

Primary, lower secondary, and upper secondary school students. Schoolers Teams compete within their own stream and are evaluated to a calibrated standard for secondary-level technical depth.

ISCED 1–3
Students Stream
ISCED Level 4–8

Post-secondary, undergraduate, master's, doctoral students, and recent graduates. Any team that includes at least one ISCED 4–8 member is classified as a Students Team.

ISCED 4–8
