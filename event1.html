About This Event

What to Expect

The Blockchain Foundations Clinic is designed for students who want a clear, jargon-free introduction to blockchain technology — whether you're preparing for IBCOL, exploring the ISFRETIC curriculum, or simply curious about how distributed systems work.

Over four hours, you'll move from first principles — what is a blockchain and why does it matter — through to hands-on exploration of smart contract logic and real-world deployment scenarios. Sessions are structured for discussion, not just lecture.

By the end of the clinic, you'll have a working mental model of how blockchain systems are designed, what problems they solve (and which they don't), and how you can engage with the technology meaningfully in a competition or research context.

Learning Outcomes

What You'll Learn

Distributed Ledgers

How distributed databases differ from centralised ones, and why that distinction matters for trust.

Consensus Mechanisms

Proof of Work, Proof of Stake, and BFT-style consensus — how networks agree without central authority.

Smart Contracts

What smart contracts are, how they execute, and why they're central to DeFi, NFTs, and enterprise use cases.

Real-World Use Cases

Supply chain, identity verification, digital finance — and how to critically evaluate blockchain proposals.

IBCOL Competition Context

How these concepts map to IBCOL judging criteria and what makes a competition-ready project proposal.

Q&A with Practitioners

Direct access to industry practitioners and KBI alumni to ask the questions your textbook won't answer.
