IBCOL HK 2025
Qualifying Round

42 teams submitted proposals across the Schoolers and Students streams. Six advanced to Finals Day. Here's a full account of how the qualifying round unfolded.

8 March 2025
HKU iDendron, Pokfulam
42 teams · 6 finalists selected
Event Recap

How the Round Unfolded

The IBCOL HK 2025 Qualifying Round took place over a single Saturday at HKU's iDendron innovation hub, Pokfulam. Forty-two teams — 28 from the Students Stream and 14 from the Schoolers Stream — presented five-minute pitches of their blockchain project proposals, followed by a ten-minute cross-examination from a panel of three judges.

The format was deliberately pressured: judges were instructed to probe technical assumptions, challenge problem statements, and test whether teams understood the limitations of their proposed approaches. The aim was not merely to identify the strongest projects, but to prepare all participants for the rigour of the international IBCOL finals circuit.

Six teams — four from the Students Stream and two from the Schoolers Stream — were selected to advance to ETO Hong Kong 2025 Finals Day in June. The remaining 36 teams received detailed written feedback and were invited to continue developing their projects for the 2026 cycle.

Key Observations

What the Judges Said

"The standard of the Schoolers Stream submissions this year genuinely surprised us. Two teams could have comfortably held their own in the Students category. That is a sign the programme is working." — Lead Judge, IBCOL Track 2025

Judges noted that the most common reason for elimination was not technical weakness, but a failure to clearly define the problem being solved. Teams that survived cross-examination typically entered the room knowing exactly what blockchain added to their solution — and what it didn't.
