ETO Hong Kong 2026
Is Now Open

KBI Hong Kong Editorial
Official KBI HK Announcement
10 May 2026
5 min read

KBI Hong Kong is pleased to announce that the Emerging Technologies Olympiad Hong Kong 2026 (ETO HK 2026) is officially open for interest registration. This marks the beginning of the 2025–2026 competition cycle — KBI HK's most anticipated annual programme.

ETO Hong Kong is the city's flagship gateway to three international olympiads: the International Blockchain Olympiad (IBCOL), the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL), and the International Quantum Computing Olympiad (IQCOL). Students who compete under ETO HK represent Hong Kong on the global stage.

"ETO HK is where Hong Kong's sharpest student minds prove themselves — not just against each other, but against the best in the world."

— KBI Hong Kong Executive Committee

What's New in 2026

The 2026 cycle brings several structural updates to how ETO HK operates. Most significantly, the programme now formally adopts a two-stream model aligned with international ISCED classification, ensuring every level of student can compete in an appropriately benchmarked cohort.

The two streams are:

  • Schoolers Stream (ISCED 1–3) — Open to primary and secondary school students. Teams in this stream are designated Schoolers Teams and produce Schoolers Projects.
  • Students Stream (ISCED 4–8) — Open to sub-degree, undergraduate, and postgraduate students. Mixed teams (Students + Schoolers) are permitted with a tertiary majority.

This change follows sustained feedback from participating schools and universities, who noted that mixed-level competition was creating an uneven playing field. The new structure ensures every team competes fairly while preserving the community's inclusive, cross-institution ethos.

ETO HK 2026 at a Glance
Three tracks: IBCOL (Blockchain), IDSOL (Data Science), IQCOL (Quantum Computing)
Two streams: Schoolers (ISCED 1–3) and Students (ISCED 4–8)
Team size: 2–5 members; multi-school teams welcome
Format: Written project paper + live presentation
Top teams advance to international finals in their respective olympiad

Three Tracks, Three International Stages

Each technology track in ETO HK 2026 is aligned to one international olympiad, giving top-performing Hong Kong teams the opportunity to represent the city on a global stage:

  • IBCOL (Blockchain Track): Teams develop a blockchain application addressing a real-world problem. The top Schoolers and Students team from HK advances to the IBCOL international competition.
  • IDSOL (Data Science Track): Teams produce a data-driven research project or applied AI solution. Top teams advance to the IDSOL international competition.
  • IQCOL (Quantum Track): Teams investigate a quantum computing algorithm or framework. Top teams advance to the IQCOL international competition.

Registration and Next Steps

Interest registration is now open. Prospective participants — individually or as a team — are encouraged to register interest now to receive:

  • The official ETO HK 2026 prospectus and competition handbook
  • Invitations to pre-competition workshops and clinics
  • Team formation support (for solo registrants seeking teammates)
  • Early-bird announcements for official registration opening

Official team registration and track selection will open in Q1 2026. Full details on submission deadlines, judging criteria, and presentation day logistics will be published in the prospectus.

"We've consistently seen that students who engage with ETO — even if they don't win — walk away with depth of understanding that sets them apart in university applications, job interviews, and research settings."

— KBI HK Programme Director

Supporting Programmes

Alongside the competition itself, KBI HK is running a series of preparatory programmes throughout the 2025–2026 cycle:

  • Blockchain Foundations Clinic (15 March 2026) — A half-day introduction to blockchain technology for students new to the field
  • Data Science Bootcamp (April 2026, TBC) — A hands-on session covering the data science pipeline from problem framing to model presentation
  • ISFRETIC Certification — Available year-round for students seeking formal credentials in blockchain, data science, or quantum computing
  • Community Meet-Ups — Quarterly networking events connecting the broader KBI HK community

All KBI members receive priority access to these preparatory events. Membership is free for students.

ETO HK 2026 interest registration is open now. Don't miss the chance to compete at Hong Kong's premier emerging technology olympiad.
